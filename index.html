What We Do

We believe technology empowers journalists and researchers to use the environment as a firsthand data source.

We are distributing cameras designed to capture coral reef images to scuba divers in order to mine data from images of coral that can help us gauge the health of reefs in these areas. This innovative idea combines new imaging modalities, crowdsourced data collection and analysis in a new way that will provide a model for participatory storytelling and citizen journalism in environmental reporting. Environmental reporting typically relies on scientific and government reports. We would like to add a new dimension to climate change stories by empowering journalists to deploy their own sensors and tools to collect data directly from the environment, enabling a new type of environmental interview. Instead of relying on experts, we are turning the environment itself into a source.

We are also focusing on stories about coral reefs because they are the “canary in the coal mine” for determining the health of ocean-based ecosystems, as well as coastal areas. Not only does a quarter of the ocean’s animals and plants depend on healthy reefs for food and shelter, but entire economies lean on the tourism and fishing industries reliant on thriving coral populations. In the Florida Keys alone, $2 to 3 billion come in every diving season because of the coral reefs just off the coast. But these reefs are in perpetual danger of bleaching events and damage from human intervention, as well as rising ocean temperatures. At the same time, current coral reef monitoring strategies tend to be slow, expensive and reactionary, targeting reefs with already noticeable damage. People’s livelihoods, hobbies, and even their health hang in the balance along with the life of the coral. Our crowdsourced image data collection and automated analysis will rapidly speed up this monitoring process, allowing journalists and scientists to identify reefs in jeopardy before a major bleaching event happens and to share that story in major media.


Who We Are

We are an interdisciplinary team between Columbia and Stanford, combining expertise in computational imaging, data science, journalistic storytelling, multimedia production, computer science, and, of course, diving.

KRISTEN M. ALTENBURGER
Data Science Lead

Kristen is a statistician with a variety of technical research experience working with organizations like Beijing Genomics Institute, Stanford Law School, LinkedIn and Sandia National Laboratories.






HENRYK BLASINSKI
Computational Imaging Lead

Henryk holds an M.S. degree in telecommunications and computer science from Lodz University of Technology, Poland, and he is currently pursuing a Ph. D. in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University. His research interests focus on imaging, computer vision and applied machine learning. Henryk hopes that the power of modern day machine vision techniques can bridge the gap between scarce, scientific quality data and crowdsourced information available at scale. Besides, how much more motivated would you feel, if the selfie you just took could actually be useful for a greater good, such as coral reef conservation efforts? In his own time Henryk is a keen landscape photographer and an avid sailor. He participated in sailing trips all over the World: San Francisco Bay Area, Caribbean, Mediterranean and the Baltic Sea.
